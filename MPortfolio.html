University of Michigan
Solar Car Team

It was the start of my third semester at UofM when I decided to join the University of Michigan Solar Car Team (henceforth known as Solar Car). Being remote, I no longer had an activity to pour efforts and long hours over, such as ballroom, so I figured I should join something that will enable me to learn a completely new skill.

Now, I am on the Aerodynamics division learning how to CAD and run computational fluid dynamics (CFD) while meeting my teammates for the next three years.

More updates to come.

Tau Chi Epsilon (TXE)

Currently on its first iteration, TXE is an organization that I founded along with two of my friends. Established in the summer of 2020, our vision is a community of self-motivated and remarkable individuals who are deeply interested in technology and its power to change the world.

Through the many obstacles the organization has faced in 2020, I now understand the difficulties of building a virtual community. And more importantly, I now understand the aspects of running an organization: many hours of behind-the-scenes work and many failed test runs.

I would like to thank our members for their patience and second-chances. For more information about TXE, feel free to check us out here.

Michigan Ballroom Dance Team

Ballroom taught me how to dance; dance when it mattered most and dance when no one is looking.
As an art, ballroom has many rules, and I have not yet learned all of them. But like all art, it is a medium of expression, and I have learned to express myself in another way.
Just to name a few lessons I have learned:

  • How to effectively communicate, sometimes even without words
  • How to show up day after day, and still have as much energy as the very first
  • How to cheer for other teammates
  • How to entrust in your partner, and the power of letting go
  • How to take criticism and reflect on your own shortcomings
  • How to smile and keep smiling

Competency: Empathy, Lifelong Learning
